Power Usage at Gonzaga

On average an American classroom utilizes 10 kilowatt-hours (kWh) of electricity per square foot on an annual basis. Our rough estimate has Gonzaga at around 100,000 square feet, meaning that each year we use a staggering 1,000,000 kilowatt-hours of electricity.

Drawing Comparisons

Drawing some comparisons, the average American household uses 10,649 kilowatt-hours of electricity per year. This means that Gonzaga uses the same amount of electricity as about 94 American households. Furthermore, if applied electric vehicles a car with a 1,000,000 kilowatt-hour battery could drive around the circumference of the world 133 times (24,901).

Water Usage Concerns

Water waste is another significant concern at Gonzaga. With manual faucets frequently left running in bathrooms and facilities across campus, we estimate that thousands of gallons of water are wasted each month. The EPA estimates that a faucet left running for just 5 minutes wastes about 2.5 gallons of water. Multiply this by hundreds of daily uses across campus, and the waste becomes substantial. This not only impacts our environmental footprint but also leads to unnecessary utility costs.
